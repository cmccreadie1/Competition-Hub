      1. WELCOME & MATCH OVERVIEW

      Slide 1 of 10

      Getting Started with Your Match Software

      Welcome to your Match Software! This platform is designed to take the stress out of running a major shore fishing competition. It completely automates the two biggest headaches of match organizing: generating a fair beach draw and calculating leaderboards. By handling all complex tie-breakers and zone mathematics natively, the software allows you to focus entirely on running a smooth match.

      The Simple Three-Step Workflow

      • STEP 1: THE DRAW SETUP – Choose your options, enter your anglers, and let the software map out a mathematically fair beach placement.
      • STEP 2: EASY SCORE ENTRY – Type catch totals directly from paper sheets into automated scorecards. The app saves instantly as you type.
      • STEP 3: LIVE LEADERBOARDS – View immaculate, real-time standings for individuals, teams, and the biggest fish instantly.

      2. COMPETITION SETUP & DETAILS

      Slide 2 of 10

      Basic Match Details & Accessibility Settings

      Setting up a brand-new match takes less than two minutes. The entire setup screen is designed to guide you step-by-step so you cannot make a mistake.

      • 1. BASIC MATCH DETAILS: Click the title area to enter the official name of your competition, then select whether this is a 1-Day Match or a 2-Day Match. The system automatically configures all scoring and leaderboard matrix grids behind the scenes based on this choice.

      2. SMART ACCESSIBILITY FUNCTION: Toggle this "ON" if you have anglers who require flat, easy-to-reach beach pegs. Once the draw runs, these specific anglers will automatically be allocated priority placements from your ruleset without disrupting random fairness for the rest of the field:

      • OPTION A: DISTRIBUTED PEGS: Use if you have suitable, easy-access pegs scattered safely along the beach (e.g., near parking). Type specific peg numbers separated by commas (like 5, 12, 19).
      • OPTION B: STATIC ZONES: Use if beach access is highly restricted and only one specific section is safe. Assign which general color zone they will fish in for each day of the match.

      3. CREATING YOUR ROSTER & LINKING ACCESS

      Slide 3 of 10

      The Unified Entry Template and Priority Flagging

      To save time and prevent layout errors during the busy morning check-in, the software features a single, unified entry roster system that directly pairs with your accessibility rules.

      • 1. SIMPLE DATA ENTRY TEMPLATE: Use the intuitive onscreen selector controls to specify the exact number of Teams and Solo Anglers fishing the match. The hub instantly builds a clean template grid directly in front of you.
      • 2. ASSIGNING ACCESSIBILITY PEGS: Look closely at the roster template list. Right beside each angler's name field, you will see a small [A] button. If an angler has a mobility constraint, simply click their [A] button. It will highlight instantly, telling the draw algorithm to treat this person as a priority entry.
      • 3. PROCEED TO DRAW: For teams, group fields together by Team Name. For solo competitors, use the standalone rows. Once all names are filled and your access priority buttons are highlighted, click the bright "Generate Draw" button. The priority anglers are automatically matched to your easy-access rules first, before the rest of the beach shuffling begins.

      4. UNDERSTANDING THE DRAW & VISUAL LEGEND

      Slide 4 of 10

      Smart Placements, Legends, and Uneven Fields

      The moment you click "Generate Draw", the math engine shuffles positions based on three strict rules of absolute sporting fairness:

      • Separated Teams: Anglers from the same team are automatically split up so they never fish in the same zone against each other.
      • No Repeat Zones: On a 2-Day match, no competitor will ever fish the same beach zone twice.
      • Priority Access Placements: Accessibility anglers are placed into their safe pegs first, before the remaining field is randomly scattered.
      • THE VISUAL PEG LEGEND: A beautiful, color-coded legend bar appears at the top of the screen. This maps out the physical beach sequence by zone, showing exactly which peg numbers belong to the Red, Yellow, Green, and Blue sections for Day 1 and Day 2. Priority pegs are clearly highlighted.
      • EXTENDED PEGS: If late entries arrive after the draw is finalized, the system creates "Extended Pegs" (like 15A). This squeezes late arrivals onto the beach elegantly without altering the random positions already drawn by other anglers.

      5. POST-DRAW MANAGEMENT & FLOATING BUTTONS

      Slide 5 of 10

      Making Adjustments and Formatting Steward Sheets

      Once the draw is live, two convenient, sticky Floating Action Buttons appear at the bottom of your screen providing total control over match operations.

      • 1. THE "MANAGE DRAW" MENU: Handle last-minute slipway changes easily:
        • Amend Names: Correct spelling typos on the fly without changing anyone's physical peg location.
        • Delete No-Shows: Remove an entry cleanly. (Team Rule: If they belong to a team, click to unlink the team into solo anglers first so the remaining members aren't penalized).
        • Add Late Entries: Squeeze late arrivals smoothly onto extended pegs (`A`) to keep the primary draw safe.
        • Full Redraw (Last Resort): Wipes the slate clean to generate a brand-new random draw in the unlikely event that so many last-minute changes have occurred that the current setup can no longer abide by the fairness rules. Note: You do not have to re-enter all the angler names and teams; the software remembers your roster and simply reshuffles assignments instantly.
      • 2. THE "OUTPUT MENU": Formats your screen into clean, printer-friendly layouts perfect for handing out:
        • Master Angler List: An alphabetical directory of every competitor in the match.
        • Team Draws: Grouped cleanly by club name so team captains can instantly see member locations.
        • Zone Steward Sheets: A sequential list of pegs broken down zone-by-zone, ideal for stewards physically tracking cards on the beach.

      6. RECORDING MATCH SCORES

      Slide 6 of 10

      Fast Keyboard Input & Real-Time Auto-Saving

      When paper catch sheets come back to the organizer's desk, entering data is fast, seamless, and completely foolproof.

      • SELECT THE ACTIVE DAY: Click the "Scorecards" tab and choose either the Day 1 or Day 2 sub-screen.
      • STICKY SEARCH BAR: Do not waste time scrolling through a massive list. Type the first few letters of an angler's name into the search box at the top to bring their scorecard straight to the front.
      • KEYBOARD TAB OPTIMIZATION: Type the 4 metrics from the steward sheet: Total Length (cm), Fish Count, Biggest Fish Size (cm), and Species Count. Hit the Tab key on your keyboard to instantly jump to the next input box, letting you log an entire card in seconds without touching your mouse.
      • INSTANT SAVING: There is no save button. The software saves your metrics the exact millisecond you type them. The leaderboards instantly recalculate in the background automatically.

      7. SECRET PAIRS SIDE-POOL

      Slide 7 of 10

      Run an Automated Blind-Draw Pool for Every Angler

      The Secret Pairs game is an optional side-pool that pairs anglers up completely at random and gives the entire field an absolutely equal chance to win prize money.

      • THE TARGET LENGTH METHOD: Instead of crowning the highest score, the computer calculates a hidden Target Length that falls strictly between the lowest and highest possible combined scores. The pair whose combined length finishes closest to the target wins, completely removing predictable elements!
      • CASH POT DETAILS: Enter the entry fee per person. The software instantly maps out the total pot and displays exact cash payouts for 1st (50%), 2nd (30%), and 3rd (20%) places. Toggle non-participants to "NO" by tapping the box next to their name to remove them from the draw pool instantly.
      • THE "JOE AVERAGE" SAFEGUARD: If you have an uneven number of entries in the cash pool, the computer automatically generates a virtual partner named Joe Average. Joe is mathematically given the exact mean average score of the entire active field. This completes the final pair perfectly and ensures no real angler is left out of the game.

      8. RESULTS, LEADERBOARDS & SCORING RULES

      Slide 8 of 10

      The Multi-Metric Calculation Engine Hierarchy

      The Results tab houses your master tournament dashboards, backed by a clear **Scoring Legend** bar at the top of the page. The field is ranked using a native priority hierarchy system to instantly resolve ties:

      • 1. PRIMARY METRIC – LOWEST ZONE POINTS: Anglers earn points based on where they placed inside their specific colored zone (1 point for 1st, 2 points for 2nd, etc.). The lowest total points wins the match.
        • The Blank Rule: Anglers who catch nothing (Blank Card) automatically receive maximum zone points equal to the total number of entries stationed inside that specific zone. Tying active scores automatically split the average points of the tied positions.
      • 2. TIE-BREAKER 1 – TOTAL LENGTH (cm): If total zone points match, the competitor with the higher cumulative fish length takes the lead.
      • 3. TIE-BREAKER 2 – TOTAL FISH COUNT: If lengths match, the angler who landed the highest number of fish wins.
      • 4. TIE-BREAKER 3 – SINGLE LARGEST FISH (cm): If points, length, and counts are identical, victory goes to the angler with the longest single catch.
      • 5. TIE-BREAKER 4 – TOTAL SPECIES COUNT: The final tier separator rewards the angler who caught the widest variety of distinct fish species.
      • CORE SCREENS PROVIDED: We provide full data grids for Zone Standings, Individual Champion Matrix, Team Championship (filters out solos automatically), and a side-by-side Top 10 Biggest Fish Board for Day 1 and Day 2.

      1. The Peg Draw: Absolute Randomness & Fairness

      This app relies on the browser’s native Pseudo-Random Number Generator (PRNG). This is a standardized, mathematically audited engine built directly into modern web browsers (like Google Chrome, Apple Safari, and Microsoft Edge).

      It is the exact same mathematical engine trusted globally for online banking encryption, secure web connections, and scientific simulations. This algorithm has been subjected to rigorous global industry testing (known as "Dieharder" statistical test suites) to guarantee three strict rules:

      • Zero Predictable Patterns: No sequence of draws can ever be predicted or reverse-engineered.
      • Zero Bias: Every single angler and every single peg has a mathematically identical probability of being selected.
      • Zero Memory: The computer doesn't "remember" past draws, ensuring each randomized calculation is completely independent of the last.

      Furthermore, the app enforces automated clash detection (ensuring team members are evenly split across zones), a complex rule-set that is virtually impossible to enforce purely by hand without bias. Once the draw is generated, the results are immediately locked and timestamped.

      2. Secret Pairs: Unpredictable & Un-riggable

      The Secret Pairs target length is completely organic and impossible to predict, influence, or manipulate, because the calculations rely entirely on live data that does not exist until the match is over.

      The target is determined by a strict, hard-coded mathematical formula that uses the actual total fish lengths caught by the competitors on the day. Because no one can predict how many fish will be caught, what species will show up, or what sizes they will be, the target is completely immune to human tampering.

      In the event of an odd number of competitors, the virtual partner (known as the "Ghost Partner" or "Joe Average") is assigned a score that is the mathematically exact average of the entire field's performance. This ensures that the unpaired angler is neither given an unfair advantage nor penalized, maintaining perfect competitive balance.

      3. Data Privacy & Local Security (A Closed System)

      Your personal data is completely safe, private, and secure. This application operates as a strict "closed-loop" system, meaning:

      • Zero External Servers: Unlike typical modern apps, this platform does not upload, transmit, or store your name, contact info, or scores to an external database or cloud server.
      • 100% Local Storage: All tournament data lives exclusively inside your device's web browser memory (localStorage). It never leaves the physical laptop or tablet running the event.
      • No Third-Party Tracking: We do not run any cookies, advertising trackers, or background analytics scripts. Your activity is completely private.
      • Complete Offline Independence: Because the app is completely self-contained, it can run entirely offline without an internet connection. Your data remains on your screen, in your control, at all times.
